# Usage Example - Proof Card 001: Customer Support Reply Workflow

## Plain-English summary

A buyer purchases a GoalOS product and needs help: they cannot find their download, they may need access support, or they may be asking about a refund. A normal AI reply can be polite but inconsistent. GoalOS turns that support task into a reusable workflow. AGIALPHA becomes useful when the improvement needs to be coordinated, reviewed, credentialed, and made publicly verifiable without exposing private buyer data.

## The proof mission

**Sponsor mission:** Improve the Customer Support Reply Workflow for GoalOS digital-product buyers.

**Goal:** Make replies clearer, safer, faster, and more reusable.

**Builder work:** Create workflow v1.0, test it on a realistic support scenario, score the output, identify weaknesses, improve the workflow to v1.1, and submit a public-safe proof note.

**Reviewer work:** Check that the workflow existed, the weakness was real, the improvement is plausible, private data is excluded, and public claims are safe.

**Output:** Proof Card 001, a proof credential, a reputation update, and a reusable support workflow.

## Why AGIALPHA is useful here

The buyer does not need crypto. AGIALPHA is used only for the proof-work network:

1. Sponsor posts the mission.
2. Builder claims it with an AGIALPHA bond.
3. Builder submits proof with a proof bond.
4. Reviewer validates with a reviewer bond.
5. Approved proof registers a Proof Card.
6. Credential and reputation update.
7. Better missions unlock.

## Why this creates demand

This example is easy to understand: everyone knows customer support can be slow or inconsistent. The demo shows that GoalOS does not merely answer a ticket. It improves the workflow, proves the improvement, and turns the result into a reusable asset that other teams can trust.

## Safe public wording

GoalOS improves workflows. AGIALPHA coordinates proof work. Ethereum records public-safe proof anchors. Private intelligence and buyer data stay off-chain. Proof Cards create trust. Credentials create reputation. Reputation unlocks better missions.
