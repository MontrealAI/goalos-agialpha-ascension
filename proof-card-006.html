RSI logic

RSI means proof-backed upgrade rights. Unproven work cannot become future operating context. Reviewed, selected, and scoped work can improve future missions.

Core rule: no proof, no upgrade rights. No review, no propagation. No rollback path, no institutional release.

Claim boundary

Experimental proof-card page. It does not claim achieved AGI, achieved superintelligence, guaranteed financial return, legal/tax/security approval, Ethereum mainnet deployment, or Kardashev Type II achievement. It defines what would need to be proven through Evidence Dockets before stronger claims are made.

FAQ

Why is this useful?

It makes a complex AI-work claim easier to inspect, review, reuse, and improve. The user sees a plain-English explanation while technical reviewers can inspect the proof route.

Where does AGIALPHA matter?

AGIALPHA matters where independent sponsors, builders, reviewers, credentials, reputation, and settlement need shared coordination.

What creates the moat?

The moat is the growing evidence graph: repeated missions, artifacts, reviews, outcomes, costs, risks, and route memory that cannot be copied overnight.
