Superintelligence is an inflow-control problem

A proof-governed intelligence organization is an open system. It must continuously receive and regulate compute, data, tasks, incentives, feedback, governance, and tools. Maximum inflow is not the goal. Regulated proof-conditioned inflow is the goal.

Without proof-regulated inflows

The system becomes idle, stale, chaotic, self-referential, overfit to internal metrics, or unsafe.

With a proof thermostat

The system can accelerate what proves itself, slow what becomes disputed, quarantine what fails, and reinvest only into replayable verified capability.

Core equations

SuperintelligencePotential ≈ ModelCapability × VerifiedExperience × ToolPower × Memory × RecursiveImprovement × Governance × InflowControl. VerifiedExperienceRate = Σ AcceptedαWU × DocketQuality × ReplayPassRate × ReusePotential ÷ (Cost + Risk + Latency + ProofDebt). ProofThermostatPolicy = f( ValidatedαWU, DocketQuality, ReplayPassRate, FalseAcceptanceRate, DisputeRate, ValidatorLatency, SafetyIncidents, RewardHackingSignals, ExternalReplayRate, ProofDebt ). InflowControl = BudgetAllocation × ComputeCredits × ValidatorSupply × ChallengeDepth × MarketPermeability × TreasuryReinvestment. Hard rule: If ClaimBoundaryFail OR QAFail OR ReplayFail OR CriticalSafetyIncident, then Settlement = 0 and Reinvestment = 0 for that mission class until repair.

Seven regulated inflows

Compute

GPU, CPU, tool, sandbox, and validation capacity.

Data

Sources, telemetry, provenance, retrieval, and outcomes.

Tasks

Missions, benchmarks, user goals, research agendas, and defects.

Incentives

Escrow, stake, rewards, penalties, access, and reinvestment.

Feedback

Tests, validators, human review, incidents, and delayed outcomes.

Governance

Policy, legal/safety boundaries, risk classes, and escalation.

Tools are the seventh inflow: browsers, APIs, code, databases, contracts, labs, and deployments. They must remain scoped, logged, permissioned, and rollbackable.
