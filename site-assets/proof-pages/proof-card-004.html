In plain English

This card introduces the persistent layer: the system is not a one-off agent demo. It is an experimental framework that keeps memory of goals, evidence, artifacts, outcomes, reviews, credentials, and what may shape future work.

Proof mission: Create a persistent proof memory that links goals, runs, artifacts, reviews, proof cards, credentials, reputation, and future routing.

Why this matters

A persistent intelligence system becomes valuable when it remembers what worked, what failed, who proved it, and which artifacts deserve reuse. This is the first step toward compounding institutional intelligence.

Recursive Self-Improvement logic

RSI is proof-backed upgrade rights. Unproven work cannot become future operating context. Replayed, reviewed, selected work can.

Claim boundary

Experimental proof-card page. It does not claim achieved AGI, achieved superintelligence, guaranteed financial return, legal/tax/security approval, Ethereum mainnet deployment, or Kardashev Type II achievement. It defines what would need to be proven through Evidence Dockets before stronger claims are made.

FAQ

Does the buyer need crypto?

No. Buyers can experience GoalOS as a product/workflow. AGIALPHA becomes useful when proof work, bonds, review, credentials, and settlement need shared coordination.

What makes this an execution moat?

The moat is not a slogan. It is the evidence graph: repeated missions, ProofBundles, reviewer attestations, red-team outcomes, cost/risk ledgers, delayed outcomes, and route memory.

How does this relate to RSI?

Artifacts improve future work only after they are proven, reviewed, selected, and scoped. That prevents unverified material from becoming future operating context.
